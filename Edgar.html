Edgar Mission

Edgar’s Mission is a not for profit sanctuary for rescued farmed animals. On the ground the animal sanctuary is bursting with visitors, however their website was kind of outdated feel.In this case study I'll show how I helped Edgar's by:

Edgar's needed:

  • Redesigning their website
  • Improving their donation and booking system
  • Increasing customer trust

The Problem

The design of the original website is cluttered, text heavy and has poor information hierarchy, together these problems made site navigation difficult. This made users hesitant to donate on the website or even use the site itself.

Research

Interview

After the initial site evaluation, we took 6 users to find out if how they felt about the site as well as answering the following

  • Q1 : How long does it take for users to donate?
  • Q2 : What are the impressions of the site?
  • Q3 : Can users find the booking section?

All interview participants mentioned few key features that they would check first; a product price, a good picture quality, description and delivery waiting time

All participants mentioned about the issue betweenprice and quality. Need encourage to shop and unclear aboutdeliver time and method.

80% users failed to navigate to vennies ebay shop and gave up because of frustration. They also mention the content information on its landing page was a bit clutted and no sense hierarc

The Solution

The Edgar’s Mission website was designed to gain support via donations but through user research, a social media audit and stakeholder interviews I discovered the best way to improve the site would be to redesign focusing on improving customer trust by improving the donations and booking experience
